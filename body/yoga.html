Yoga: Ancient Asana, AI Precision

Six foundational yoga poses with alignment cues, modifications, and AI coaching prompts. Whether you're a complete beginner or a returning practitioner, this is your guide.

The word asana in Sanskrit means "comfortable seat" ‚Äî a position in which you can be at ease. True yoga is not about contorting into pretzel shapes for Instagram. It's about finding the edge between effort and ease, learning to breathe through discomfort, and cultivating a conscious relationship with your physical form.

AI alignment guidance takes the guesswork out of self-practice. The prompts below give you what a yoga teacher would say ‚Äî adapted for your level, body type, and intention.

Tadasana ‚Äî Mountain Pose

Beginner Foundation

The mother of all standing poses. Stand with feet hip-width apart, ground through all four corners of each foot, lift your kneecaps, draw your navel gently in, roll shoulders back and down, crown of head reaching skyward.

AI Alignment Tip: Imagine a golden thread pulling the crown of your head upward while your feet root like tree roots into the earth. Breathe into your chest ‚Äî notice if you're collapsing or overarching. Find the neutral.

Vrikshasana ‚Äî Tree Pose

Beginner Balance

From Mountain, shift weight to your left foot. Place the right foot on the inner left thigh or calf (never on the knee). Bring hands to heart center or raise overhead. Fix your gaze on a still point. Hold for 5‚Äì8 breaths.

AI Alignment Tip: If you wobble, press your foot and inner thigh firmly together ‚Äî they are co-creating stability. Soften the face and jaw. Tension travels. A relaxed face = a more stable tree.

Adho Mukha Svanasana ‚Äî Downward Dog

Beginner Full Body

From hands and knees, tuck toes, lift hips high and back. Spread fingers wide, press through all fingertips. Lengthen the spine by pressing hips toward the ceiling. Bend knees if hamstrings are tight ‚Äî that's perfectly fine.

AI Alignment Tip: The goal is a long spine, not straight legs. Externally rotate your upper arms to keep elbows from splaying. Breathe deeply into your side ribs ‚Äî you'll feel the pose deepen with each exhale.

AI Alignment Tip: Warrior One is about the hips ‚Äî they want to open sideways, but the pose demands they face forward. Think of drawing your back hip forward and front hip back. Feel the difference. That engagement is the pose.

Bhujangasana ‚Äî Cobra Pose

Beginner Backbend

Lie face down, place palms under shoulders. Press the tops of your feet into the mat. On an inhale, peel the chest off the floor, using your back muscles more than your arms. Elbows can be slightly bent. Lengthen through the crown.

AI Alignment Tip: If you feel pinching in the lower back, you've gone too high. Come down until you find a comfortable depth. The cobra should feel like opening, not compressing. Broaden through your collarbones.

Balasana ‚Äî Child's Pose

Beginner Restoration

From kneeling, bring big toes together, knees wide as hips or wider. Fold forward, extending arms overhead or resting them alongside your body. Let your forehead rest on the mat. This is your resting place ‚Äî return here whenever needed.

AI Alignment Tip: Child's Pose is not failure ‚Äî it is wisdom. Practice staying here for 2 full minutes with intentional diaphragmatic breathing. Feel your lower back rise and fall with each breath. Let the floor hold you completely.

For Beginners

1

2

3

4

5

6

Rest, integrate, breathe. Let the practice settle into your body.
