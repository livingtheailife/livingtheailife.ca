The Philosophy

Functional Fitness: Moving for Life

Our ancestors didn't exercise ‚Äî they moved. Hunting, farming, building, carrying, climbing, swimming. Their bodies were shaped by necessity. Our modern challenge is to create that variety and challenge intentionally.

Functional movement trains the body for real-world activities: picking things up from the floor (deadhinge), getting up from sitting (squat), carrying things (loaded carry), pushing and pulling (horizontal and vertical patterns), rotating through the hips and spine (rotation), and moving in all directions (locomotion).

These are the seven movement patterns that, when trained consistently, produce a body that is capable, resilient, and pain-free well into advanced age.

Movement: Romanian Deadlift or Kettlebell Swing
